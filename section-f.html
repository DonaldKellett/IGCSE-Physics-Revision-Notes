Explain what is a hard magnetic material and give an example of something that would be made from a hard magnetic material. A hard magnetic material is one that retains its magnetism once it has been magnetised. An example would be (permanent) bar magnets.
Explain what is a soft magnetic material and give an example of something that would be made from a soft magnetic material. A soft magnetic material is one that loses its magnetism easily. An example would be an electromagnet.
When North and South poles are placed near to each other, there is a(n) _______________________ magnetic field. (almost) uniform
Draw a sketch of the field lines you would expect when a North Pole is placed very near a South Pole.
Field lines always go from _________ to ___________. (in order) North, South
When a current passes through a wire, given that you known the direction of the current, you determine the direction of the magnetic field generated by using the _____________________ rule. right hand grip
In the rule mentioned above, the direction in which the thumb is pointing is the direction of the current/magnetic field. current
Explain how an electric bell works. When the switch (bell push) is pressed, the circuit is complete and current flows. The soft iron core of the electromagnet becomes magnetised and attracts the iron armature. When the armature moves, the hammer strikes the bell and at the same time a gap is created at the contact screw. The circuit is incomplete and current stops flowing. The electromagnet is now turned off so the spring's armature returns to its original position. The circuit is again complete and the whole process repeats itself again.
Explain how a circuit breaker works. If the current is too large, the electromagnet becomes strong enough to pull the iron catch out of position so the contacts open and the circuit is open (breaks). Once the problem has been fixed, the catch is repositioned by pressing the reset button.
Explain how a relay switch works. When the switch is closed the circuit is closed and therefore a (small) current flows around the circuit where the switch is located. As current passes through the coil surrounding the electromagnet, the soft iron core becomes magnetised and attracts the iron armature, which is pivoted at a point. As a result, the lower end of the armature pushes the contacts of the second circuit (with a much higher voltage) together, which completes the second circuit so current flows in the second circuit without the user ever coming into contact with that more dangerous circuit. If the switch is open, the (first) circuit is open and so no current flows. The electromagnet is switched off (i.e. it loses its magnetism), the iron armature returns to its original position and the contacts at the second circuit spring apart, so that circuit is also open and no current flows.
In what conditions is a voltage induced in a wire (assuming you also have a magnet of some sort)? A voltage is induced in a wire if the wire is cutting magnetic field lines (at different rates).
When you know the direction of the current in a wire that is in a magnetic field, given that you also know the direction of the field, how do you determine the direction of the force acting on the wire? Give the name of the rule that you would use and describe how the rule works. You determine the direction of the force acting on the wire by using Fleming's Left Hand Rule (also known as the Motor Rule). The thumb is the direction of the force acting on the wire, the index finger is the direction of the magnetic field and the middle finger is the direction of the current.

A step-up transformer increases the current/voltage, given that it is 100% efficient. voltage
A step-down transformer increases the current/voltage, given that it is 100% efficient. current (since voltage is stepped down and total power input = total power output)
A step-up transformer has more turns on the primary/secondary coil. secondary
A step-down transformer has more turns on the primary/secondary coil. primary
In the UK national grid, a step-up/step-down transformer is used to reduce 'loss' of electrical energy due to resistance. step-up
