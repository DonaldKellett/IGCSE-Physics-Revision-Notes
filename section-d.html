Key Definitions and Concepts

In this table, the words in bold are the words that are most likely to get you the marks in the exam.

Keyword/Concept Definition [Show] [Hide]
Name three types of energy. (any three from) Light Energy, Sound Energy, Thermal (heat) energy, GPE, KE, Electrical Energy, Elastic Potential Energy, Chemical Energy
The law of conservation of energy states that ... Energy is neither created nor destroyed. (It is just converted from one form to another)
The useful energy output is always greater than/equal to/less than the total energy input. less than
Define conduction. Thermal conduction is the transfer of thermal energy through a substance without the substance itself moving.
Define convection. Thermal convection is the transfer of thermal energy through a fluid (liquid/gas) by the upward movement of warmer, less dense regions of fluid.
Define radiation. Thermal radiation is the transfer of energy by IR waves.
In a snowy day, the roofs of most houses are covered in snow. However, the snow on the roof of one particular house has melted. What does this suggest about the insulation of that particular house and why? It suggests that the insulation in that house is poor, because the melted snow suggests that a lot of heat is being lost through the roof (by conduction).
Briefly explain how double glazing insulates a house. When double glazing is used, a thin layer of insulating air is trapped between the two sheets of glass, thus reducing heat loss by conduction. Also, since the gap between the panes are not wide enough for convection currents to form, heat loss by convection is also prevented.
Define energy in terms of work. Energy is the ability to do work.
Define non-renewable resource. A non-renewable energy resource is one that effectively cannot be replaced once it has been used.
Give an example of a non-renewable resource. (any one from) coal, oil, natural gas, nuclear fuel
Define renewable resource. A renewable energy resource is one that will not run out.
Give an example of a renewable resource. (any one from) hydroelectric power, tidal power, wave energy, wind power, solar power, geothermal energy
Describe the energy conversions/transfers that occur in a wind turbine. At first, kinetic energy in the wind is converted to rotational kinetic energy in the wind turbine. It then is converted to electrical energy in a generator.

Exam-Style Question

A hotel has escalators to move people between floors.

(a) A woman of mass 70kg steps on to an escalator, which lifts her to a height of 5.5m.

(i) State the equation linking gravitational potential energy, mass, g and height. (1 mark)

(ii) Show that the gravitational potential energy gained by the woman is 4000J to one significant figure. (2 marks)

(iii) State the work done on the woman and give the unit. (2 marks)

(b) The escalator is powered by an 8kW electric motor.

(i) State the equation linking efficiency, useful energy output and total energy input. (1 mark)

(ii) The escalator lifts 25 people each minute. Each person has a mass of 70kg. Calculate the efficiency of the escalator. (3 marks)

Mark Scheme

[Show][Hide]

Key: One tick on the mark scheme is equivalent to one mark.

Part (a)(i):

  • GPE = m × g × h OR Gravitational Potential Energy = Mass × Gravitational Field Strength × Height (o.e.)

Part (a)(ii):

  • Correct Substitution of Values: GPE = 70kg × 10N/kg × 5.5m
  • Correct Answer: 3850J (~ 4000J)

Part (a)(iii):

  • 3850 (accept 4000)
  • J/joules

Part (b)(i):

  • Efficiency = Useful Energy Output⁄Total Energy Input

Part (b)(ii):

  • Calculation and correct answer for total energy input: 8000W × 60s = 480000J (or 480kJ)
  • Calculation and correct answer for useful energy output: 25 × 700N × 5.5m = 96250J
  • Calculation and correct answer for efficiency: 96250J⁄480000J = 0.201 (3 s.f.) OR 20.1% (3 s.f.)
