Exam-Style Question

A person, Anita, dropped a ball of mass 10kg.

(a) Calculate the weight, in newtons, of the ball. (2 marks)

(b) Draw a ball. Add a labelled arrow to show the weight of the ball and another labelled arrow to show another force acting on the ball during its fall through the air. State the name of the other force. (3 marks)

(c) Eventually the ball reached terminal velocity when it was travelling at constant speed. Explain why the ball reached terminal velocity. (3 marks)

Mark Scheme

[Show][Hide]

Key: One tick on the mark scheme is equivalent to one mark.

Part (a):

  • Attempts to show that 1kg = 10N (can be implied)
  • 100N

Part (b):

  • An arrow that starts from the ball (i.e. one end TOUCHES the ball), points downwards and is labelled 'W', 'Weight' (o.e.)
  • An arrow that starts from the ball (i.e. one end TOUCHES the ball), points upwards and is labelled 'Air resistance' or 'drag'
  • Air resistance/drag

Part (c):

  • As velocity increases, air resistance/drag increases
  • Eventually the two forces acting in opposite directions balance out
  • Resultant force is zero; stop accelerating/travel at constant speed - this is terminal velocity
