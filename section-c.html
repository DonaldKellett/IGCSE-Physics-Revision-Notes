Exam-Style Question

(a)(i) Explain what is meant by a digital signal. (2 marks)

(ii) Suggest two ways a digital signal could be made to carry more information. (2 marks)

(b) The frequency of an electromagnetic wave was 3280kHz and the wavelength was 91.5m.

(i) State the equation linking wave speed, frequency and wavelength. (1 mark)

(ii) Calculate the speed of the electromagnetic wave. (2 marks)

(c) Another electromagnetic wave has half of the frequency of the wave mentioned above: 1640kHz. State the wavelength of this new wave. (1 mark)

Key: One tick on the mark scheme is equivalent to one mark.

Part (a)(i):

  • 2 (distinct) values
  • only (dependent on first mark)

Part (a)(ii):

Choose any two from:

  • increase the frequency
  • regenerating the signal
  • increasing bandwidth
  • additional signal level
  • multiplexing (using more than one channel)

Part (b)(i):

  • v = f × λ OR wave speed = frequency × wavelength

Part (b)(ii):

  • Substitution of values into equation (i.e. v = 3,280,000Hz × 91.5m)
  • 300,120,000 m/s OR 300,000,000 m/s OR 3 × 108 m/s
