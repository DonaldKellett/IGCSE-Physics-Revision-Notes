Key Definitions and Concepts

Keyword/Concept Definition [Show] [Hide]
To measure the volume of an irregularly-shaped object, use a _____________________. displacement vessel
Describe how to use the apparatus mentioned above to measure the volume of an irregularly-shaped object. First set up a displacement vessel and a measuring cylinder. Place the measuring cylinder right below the outlet of the displacement vessel. After that, fill the displacement vessel with water until it just overflows. Empty any water already present in the measuring cylinder. After that, if the object is denser than water, simply lower the object to be tested (carefully) into the displacement vessel and take the reading from the measuring cylinder. The volume of water displaced is the volume of the irregularly-shaped object. If the object is less dense than water, first use an object that is more dense than water and measure the volume of that object using the method mentioned above. After that, tie the object (now with known volume) to the object that is less dense than water and allow the two objects to sink into the displacement vessel. Take the reading on the measuring cylinder. The new reading subtracted by the reading of the object more dense than water is the volume of the object that is less dense than water. Note that the width of the object must not be larger than the diameter of the displacement vessel; otherwise the object cannot be lowered.
Pressure in fluids ... act equally in all directions
Air pressure is about _______Pa. 100,000 OR 1 × 105
In a fluid, pressure increases/decreases with depth. increases
Describe the movement of particles in a solid. In a solid, the particles vibrate about fixed positions.
Describe the arrangement of particles in a solid. In a solid, the particles are packed very close together arranged in a regular (lattice) structure/pattern.
In which state of matter do the particles have the most kinetic energy? gas
Describe the movement of particles in a liquid. In a liquid, the particles vibrate and slide past each other.
Describe the arrangement of particles in a liquid. In a liquid, the particles are close together, but there is no regular pattern (overall).
Describe the movement of particles in a gas. In a gas, the particles vibrate and move around freely.
Describe the arrangement of particles in a gas. In a gas, the particles are very far apart from each other and there is no regular pattern at all.
Define Brownian motion. Brownian motion refers to the constant motion of particles in a fluid (usually in a gas) in random directions. Due to this random motion, particles in a fluid constantly collide with each other and change direction.
In absolute zero, particles have full/lots of/moderate/little/no kinetic energy. no
To convert from Kelvin to Celsius ... subtract 273 (the actual number is subtract 273.15, but as per the I/GCSE specification, 273 is the accepted answer)
To convert from Celsius to Kelvin ... add 273 (273.15, to be exact)
At absolute zero (assuming the gas is an 'ideal gas'), the gas exerts _________ pressure on the walls of a container. zero
When using the equation p1 ⁄ T1 = p2 ⁄ T2, temperature must be measured in Celsius/Kelvin/Fahrenheit/(other). Kelvin
What is 0°C in Kelvin? 273K (273.15K to be exact)
What is 0K in Celsius? -273°C (-273.15°C to be exact)

Exam-Style Question

(a) Use ideas about particles to explain why evaporation leads to cooling. (3 marks)

(b)(i) Suggest why a runner might cool down too quickly if he does not wear a foil sheet. (2 marks)

(ii) Explain how the foil sheet reduces heat loss. (2 marks)

Mark Scheme

[Show][Hide]

Key: One tick on the mark scheme is equivalent to one mark.

Part (a):

Any 3 of:

  • Evaporation is when a liquid becomes a gas/vapour
  • Idea that particles with more KE evaporate
  • Idea that it reduces average KE of particles left
  • thus reducing temperature

Part (b)(i):

  • Mention evaporation/person still covered in sweat
  • not generating as much 'new' heat

Part (b)(ii):

Either:

  • barrier to reduce particle movement
  • reducing convection/evaporation

Or:

  • shiny surface is a good reflector/poor absorber of IR radiation
  • reducing heat loss by radiation
