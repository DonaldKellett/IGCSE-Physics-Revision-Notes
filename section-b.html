Key Definitions and Concepts

In this table, the words in bold are the words that are most likely to get you the marks in the exam.

Keyword/Concept Definition [Show] [Hide]
Name 2 safety devices that shut off the electricity in a circuit if the current in them becomes too large. Fuse, Circuit Breaker
The live wire is _________, the neutral wire is _________ and the earth wire is ________________. (in order) brown, blue, green with yellow stripes
The current in the mains supply is ____________ __________. alternating current/a.c.
Mains electricity in the UK is ___ volts. 230
Describe how a fuse blows. The wire in the fuse gets hot and melts.
When rubbing electrical insulators together to charge them, ________ are transferred from one object to the other. _________ have a __________ charge. (in order) electrons, electrons, negative
Explain an advantage of giving all droplets the same charge in electrostatic painting. Since like charges repel, the paint droplets spread out in a fine mist.
Static electricity can cause ... sparks, an electrical fire, explosions (o.e.)
To measure current, connect the ________ in series/parallel. (in order) ammeter, series
To measure voltage, connect the ________ in series/parallel. (in order) voltmeter, parallel
In a series circuit, ________ is the same and ________ is shared between all components. In a parallel circuit, ________ is the same and __________ is shared between all components. (in order) current, voltage, voltage, current
Describe an advantage of parallel circuits over series circuits. In series circuits, if one component is broken, the circuit is open and all the other components fail. In parallel circuits, even if one component is broken, only that branch is affected. OR In series circuits, there is no independent control. In parallel circuits, each branch can be controlled individually by a switch.
For thermistors, resistance increases/decreases with temperature. decreases

Exam-Style Question

(a) The diagram below shows some common electrical symbols.

(i) Which symbol represents a bulb? (1 mark)

(ii) Which symbol represents a variable resistor? (1 mark)

(b) A kettle connected to the mains supply takes a current of 7.15A.

(i) Which of these fuses should be used with the kettle? (1 mark)

  • 3A
  • 5A
  • 7A
  • 13A

(ii) Explain your answer. (1 mark)

(iii) The kettle needs an earth wire connection in the plug. However, another appliance, the hairdryer, has a plastic case so there is no need for an earth wire connection in the plug. Explain why the hairdryer is still safe to use. (2 marks)
